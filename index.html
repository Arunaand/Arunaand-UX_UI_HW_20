Redesign Federal Website: Case Study

Federal Communications Commission



Redesigning FCC website

How can users quickly find information on the FCC website?

In order to enhance user experience and facilitate an effective information search process, it is recommended to conduct a comprehensive redesign of the FCC website. The proposed redesign should prioritize user-friendliness, intuitive navigation, and streamlined access to pertinent information. These improvements will enable users to easily and quickly locate the information they require.

User Persona

Aspirations: wants to transition from working as an editor to owing a cable company Pain points Regulatory Concerns. Even though the cable industry has been deregulated, there are still plenty of regulatory hoops you'll need to jump through, including an FCC (Federal Communications Commission) license and a Cable Operations and Licensing System (COALS) account. New competitors have emerged, challenging the legacy systems. Cable companies must navigate a complex regulatory environment, including compliance with Federal Communications Commission (FCC) guidelines, local franchise agreements, and other industry-specific regulations, which can be time-consuming and costly. Regulatory hurdles. Financial strain .



Heuristic Evaluation

Upon conducting a heuristic evaluation of both the desktop and mobile versions of the FCC website, I observed several significant issues with the current site. Primarily, there was an excessive amount of textual content, overwhelming users. Additionally, the absence of a help button hindered users who might have become lost or confused while navigating the site. Lastly, the placement of elements, including images and text, seemed haphazard and lacked a deliberate and organized arrangement on the page.



Definition & Ideation

Upon the completion of our comprehensive research and thorough identification of the core problem, we engaged in a collaborative brainstorming session to determine which features would most effectively cater to the needs of our users. Subsequently, we endeavored to translate this experience into an emotionally-driven user journey map and storyboard. To gain a deeper understanding of our users' needs, goals, and emotions, I meticulously crafted an empathy map based on my findings. Based on Anisha's preferences, I created a competitive matrix to appraise the importance range to both the user and FCC. This deliberate approach enabled us to prioritize specific adjustments to the site over others, thereby enhancing our efficiency and productivity.



Usability tests on current site

I conducted five usability tests of the current site to see what Anisha, my proto proto-persona, would go through if I gave her five tasks.All users completed their tasks, but for some tasks it took a while for the user to be able to complete the tasks. There was a lot of information on the website, and the users found it challenging to maneuver through. I combined my notes from these tests into a diagram and created a affinity diagram of sorts.Followed by that, I went through the list to make the necessary changes to the redesign.

Wireframing & Prototyping

After finalizing the sitemap, I transitioned to wireframing the navigation and home page of the FCC website. These simplified visuals allowed for me to do five second usability tests on the home page as well.



UI Style

The colors and icons were added to the wireframe, and was tested. Based on the feedback, iterations were done.



Hi-Fi Frames & Prototyping

By combining my style guide with the low-fidelity wireframes, I were able to create high-fidelity prototypes that showcase the visual aesthetic and design elements while keeping true to the feedback from my beginning usability tests of the original site. This integration ensures that the prototype reflects the desired look and feel of the final product, providing a realistic representation of the user experience.

Conclusions & Next Steps

Lessons Learned

Redesigning an existing website is challenging.

  • Heuristic evaluation and annotations can be difficult.
  • Defining the mood for a government website is challenging.
  • Prioritizing a clean, user-friendly, and enjoyable webpage is crucial.
In summary, redesigning a government website was tough but rewarding. High-fidelity designs helped me dive deeper. The heuristic evaluation and annotations were challenging but became more satisfying to produce as I practiced more and more. Creating a mood and developing a style for a government website was difficult. However, I'm proud of my clean, professional, and fun web page.

Next steps

In order to enhance the user experience, we are committed to making iterative improvements to the navigation and home page UI elements. To achieve this, we will conduct additional usability tests, including 5-second usability tests, to obtain user feedback that will inform our iterative approach. Additionally, we plan to undertake website expansion by creating more pages that delve into the various topics featured on the home page, thereby providing our users with more content to explore beyond just the home page and navigation. Our focus on iterative improvements is aimed at ensuring that our users have an optimal user experience, which is critical to us. This approach will enable us to make informed decisions based on user feedback and to deliver a website that is user-friendly, intuitive, and engaging. In conclusion, we are committed to ensuring that our website is the best it can be, and we believe that our iterative approach, combined with user feedback, will enable us to achieve this goal.
